According to Ayurveda, every individual has a unique constitution called Prakriti, which is determined by the balance of three doshas: Vata, Pitta, and Kapha. Vata governs movement and communication, Pitta controls digestion and metabolism, while Kapha provides structure and immunity. Understanding your dominant dosha helps in choosing the right foods, lifestyle practices, and treatments for optimal health.

üå± Turmeric: The Golden Healer

Turmeric, known as Haridra in Sanskrit, is one of the most revered herbs in Ayurveda. Its active compound curcumin possesses powerful anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Regular consumption helps balance all three doshas, supports liver function, promotes healthy digestion, and enhances immunity. Mix half a teaspoon with warm milk and honey before bedtime for best results.

üçÉ Daily Routine (Dinacharya)

Ayurveda emphasizes the importance of Dinacharya or daily routine for maintaining health. Wake up before sunrise, practice oil pulling with sesame oil, perform gentle yoga or pranayama, eat your largest meal at midday when digestive fire is strongest, and wind down with meditation in the evening. This routine helps synchronize your body with natural circadian rhythms.
